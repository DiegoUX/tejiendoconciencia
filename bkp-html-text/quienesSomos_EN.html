We have been scientists -ecologists- for more than 15 years; we founded Tejiendo conCiencia with the idea of taking an environmental message to the general public in a way different from the way science does.

We founded Tejiendo conCiencia in April 2012 because of an environmental concern. For years we have crocheted as a hobby with classic stitches and classic threads, but in the face of the global problem of solid waste, we decided it was time to make our contribution to the planet and pool together our two passions: science and crochet, through design.

We make our own “threads” from materials that are no longer used such as plastic bags, cassette and VHS tapes, fabric pieces, among others, that we crochet. The products that we make are various, mainly baskets, tote bags, pencil/device cases, and mobiles. In addition, we offer services such as conferences and workshops where we create awareness about individual responsible consumption and we teach how to cut and crochet plastic bags.

Tejiendo conCiencia is today an organization through which we create awareness and we give tools to all sectors of society using the commercialization of our products and services as a way of transmitting and supporting our entrepreneurship.

Mission

To promote the responsible consumption of plastic through crochet.

FOUNDERS

Agustina Malizia

Doctor in Biological Sciences, graduated from the National University of Tucumán (UNT), Argentina, in 2007. After her postdoc, she was accepted as a researcher at the National Council of Scientific and Technical Research (CONICET) in 2010. She is now an Adjunct Researcher at the Institute of Regional Ecology, CONICET- UNT. Her main studies are related to vegetation dynamics in mature and secondary forests at the San Javier mountain ranges and its relationship with climate change, land use change and exotic invasive species. In Tejiendo conCiencia she is the Executive Coordinator.

Carolina Monmany

She obtained her MSc degree in Biology in 2004 and her doctorate in Biology in 2012, both at the University of Puerto Rico (UPR). She was professor and coordinator of Organismal Biology at the UPR between 2012 and 2014. She was accepted as a researcher at CONICET in 2016. She is now an Assistant Researcher at the Institute of Regional Ecology - CONICET, UNT. She studies the influences of landscape modifications on insect species diversity and their ecological functions. In Tejiendo conCiencia she is the Human Resources Coordinator.
